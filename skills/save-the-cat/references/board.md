# The Board (40-Card Method)

The Board lets you "see" your movie before writing pages.

## Setup

- **40 scene cards** total (final version)
- **4 horizontal rows**:
  1. Act One (Setup)
  2. Act Two A (Fun & Games)
  3. Act Two B (Bad Guys Close In)
  4. Act Three (Finale)
- **9-10 cards per row**

## What Goes on Each Card

- **Scene heading** (INT./EXT. + location + time)
- **Simple action line** (one declarative sentence)
- **+/-** (emotional change)
- **><** (conflict)

## How to Use It

1. Pin your **must-have** scenes first.
2. Place **hinge points** (Act breaks, Midpoint, All Is Lost).
3. Fill the blanks with connective tissue.
4. Look for **black holes** (gaps you can't explain).

## Color-Coding

Use different ink colors to track A-story vs. B-story.
This reveals imbalance, redundancy, and missing turns.

## Common Board Fixes

- **Too many cards in a row** -> slow pacing.
- **No conflict on cards** -> add opposition.
- **No +/- change** -> scene doesn't earn its place.
- **Clumps of one storyline** -> weave A/B more evenly.
