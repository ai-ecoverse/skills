# High-Concept "What Is It?"

High concept is the fast, visual, instantly graspable movie idea. If someone asks "What is it?", your answer should create a movie in their head.

## The Core Test

**Say it in one sentence.** If the listener doesn't immediately "see" the movie, the concept is soft.

## What Makes a Concept Sellable

- **Immediate clarity**: The hook is obvious and visual.
- **Irony baked in**: There's a built-in contradiction or twist.
- **Big poster**: You can imagine the one-sheet.
- **A trailer worth of scenes**: The idea implies set pieces.
- **Internationally legible**: The premise travels and is easy to grasp.

## "Same Thing... Only Different"

The concept should feel familiar yet fresh. Use comps to check:
- "It's *Die Hard* in a ___."
- "It's *Groundhog Day* meets ___."

If you can't find a clean "X meets Y," your idea may be muddy.

## The "What Is It?" Pitch Drill

Practice this:
1. Lead with the **hook** (irony).
2. State the **hero** and their primal **goal**.
3. Add the **situation** that makes it unique.
4. End with **stakes**.

If you can't do this in one breath, tighten.

## Pre-Sold vs. Original

Pre-sold (sequels, remakes, adaptations) reduces "What is it?" friction.  
Original ideas must be extra clear and high-concept to compete.

## Red Flags (Soft Concept)

- Needs long backstory to explain
- No clear visual hook
- Hard to imagine the poster
- "It's about..." without conflict or urgency
